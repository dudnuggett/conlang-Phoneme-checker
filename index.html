In IPA, syllables are generally separated by a period ⟨.⟩, with stress marked before the syllable using a high vertical line ⟨ˈ⟩ for primary and low line ⟨ˌ⟩ for secondary. Each vowel or diphthong marks a new syllable. Syllable breaks often follow the maximum onset principle (e.g., /əˈstrɒn.ə.mɪk.əl/).
